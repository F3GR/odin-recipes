Penovani khachapuri

This type of khachapuri is believed to have originated in the mountainous regions of Georgia. It is a layered bread is made with a flaky pastry dough and traditionally filled with cheese (sulguni or imeruli) and eggs but it may include other ingredients.

Penovani khachapuri is a delicious and comforting dish that is loved by locals and visitors alike. It is often served at special occasions, such as weddings and holidays.

Recipe

Ingredients (for 10 Servings)

  • 1 kg unsweetened puff pastry, thawed
  • 250 g of imeruli cheese, or bryndza cheese
  • 250 g of sulguni cheese, or mozzarella
  • 2 eggs
  • ½ cup wheat flour
  • a bit of milk

Preparation

  1. After preheating the oven to 180-200 °C, mix one egg and grated cheeses, then divide the mixture into 10 portions.
  2. Dust the working surface with some flour. Roll out the puff pastry, cut into 10 squares, and top them with some cheese filling. Fold the pastry into triangles or squares. Mix some milk with one egg and lightly brush the pastries.
  3. Dust a baking tray with some flour, add the pastries, and bake for 20-30 minutes, until crunchy, puffed, and golden brown. Let cool for 15 minutes before serving.
