Adjarian khachapuri

Description

Adjarian khachapuri, originating from the Adjara region of Georgia, is one of the most popular variations of Georgian khachapuri. This delectable treat takes the form of a boat-shaped open-faced bread, generously filled with cheese, commonly sulguni or imeruli, butter, and a semi-raw egg.

Traditionally, Adjarian khachapuri is savored using a fork and knife. It is often enjoyed as a breakfast dish or a satisfying snack, cherished not only within Georgia but also beyond its borders.

Recipe

Ingredients (for 4 Servings)

  • 3 cups of flour
  • 1 cup of lukewarm water
  • 2 teaspoons of active dry yeast
  • 1 teaspoon of sugar
  • 1 teaspoon of salt
  • 2 cups of grated Georgian cheese (sulguni or imeruli)
  • 4 large eggs
  • butter, to pat on top

Preparation

  1. Combine lukewarm water, sugar, and active dry yeast. Let it sit for 5-10 minutes until the yeast becomes frothy.
  2. In a large mixing bowl, combine the flour and salt. Gradually pour in the yeast mixture while stirring. Mix until the dough starts to come together.
  3. Transfer the dough to a floured surface and knead it for about 8-10 minutes until it becomes smooth and elastic. Shape the dough into a ball and place it in a greased bowl. Cover the bowl with a clean kitchen towel and let the dough rise in a warm place for about 1-2 hours.
  4. Preheat the oven to 475°F (245°C). Line a baking sheet with parchment paper. In a separate bowl, combine the grated cheese and melted butter. Mix well until the cheese is coated with butter.
  5. Once the dough has risen, punch it down to release any air bubbles. Divide the dough into 4 equal portions.
  6. Take one portion of the dough and roll it into an oval shape, approximately 1/4 inch thick. Leave the edges slightly thicker than the center.
  7. Put the rolled dough onto the prepared baking sheet. Spoon a 1/4th of the cheese mixture into the center of the dough, leaving a border around the edges.
  8. Fold the edges of the dough over the cheese mixture, partially covering it and forming a boat-like shape. Pinch the edges. Repeat steps 6-8 for 3 more times.
  9. Place the baking sheet in the preheated oven and bake for 12-15 minutes. Put an egg and place it on each of the pastries at 8-12 minutes's mark (on your preference).
  10. Remove the khachapuri from the oven, put an piece of butter on top and let them cool for a few minutes. Serve warm and enjoy!
