Satsivi

Ingredients (for 5 servings)

  • 1 whole medium-sized chicken, cut into pieces
  • 2 cups of walnuts
  • 2 large onions, chopped
  • 4 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 1 teaspoon of ground coriander
  • 1/2 teaspoon of ground fenugreek
  • 1/2 teaspoon of ground turmeric
  • 1/4 teaspoon of ground cinnamon
  • 1/4 teaspoon of ground cloves
  • 1/4 teaspoon of cayenne pepper (optional)
  • 3 teaspoons of white wine vinegar
  • 3 tablespoons of vegetable oil
  • salt, to taste
  • fresh cilantro or parsley, for garnish

Preparation

  1. In a large pot, add the chicken pieces and cover with water. Bring it to a boil over medium heat and cook for about 20-25 minutes until the chicken is fully cooked. Remove the chicken from the pot and set it aside to cool. Reserve the chicken broth.
  2. In a food processor or blender, add the walnuts and pulse until finely ground. Set it aside.
  3. In a large skillet or saucepan, heat the vegetable oil over medium heat. Add the chopped onion and saut√© until it becomes translucent and slightly golden, about 5-7 minutes. Add the minced garlic and continue cooking for an additional minute.
  4. Reduce the heat to low and add the ground walnuts to the skillet. Stir well to combine with the onion and garlic mixture.
  5. In a small bowl, mix together the ground coriander, fenugreek, turmeric, cinnamon, cloves, cayenne pepper, and salt. Add this spice mixture to the skillet and stir to coat the walnut mixture evenly.
  6. Gradually pour in the chicken broth, stirring continuously to create a smooth sauce. Bring the mixture to a simmer and cook for about 15-20 minutes, stirring occasionally, until the sauce thickens slightly.
  7. While the sauce is simmering, shred the cooled chicken into bite-sized pieces, discarding the skin and bones.
  8. Add the shredded chicken to the sauce and stir well to coat each piece with the walnut mixture. Cook for an additional 5 minutes to heat the chicken through.
  9. Remove the satsivi from the heat and stir in the white wine vinegar. Taste and adjust the seasoning with salt, if needed.
  10. Serve on a dish with fresh cilantro or parsley. Bon appetit!
